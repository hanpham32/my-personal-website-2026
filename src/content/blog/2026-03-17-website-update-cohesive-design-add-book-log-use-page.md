I've been working on revamping my personal website this week.

This time, I paid more attention to the styling of the website.
I love minimalistic theme and intentional design, so this website is
the result of that.

I also added a few more pages to decorate this corner of the internet a little more.
That includes a book log, and other misc pages like a [use page](https://hanspham.com/uses), where I share what tools and system I use on the daily.

I spent a full day learning how to use Blender and made a 3D book model.
Honestly, I didn't know I was needing that much time because I thought that if
I follow a 5 minutes Youtube video, I will be able to have my own model by
the end time. The most challenging part was UV mapping. Super stoked to see how
it turned out with the book cover images applied for the model.
I still don't know how the bulk of the different properties in
Blender is about. I know that Blender is such a powerful tool and there could be
so many more things I can make out of it.

I'm still figuring out what my website mascot should be.
For the time being, it's an otter. I think they are cute and adorable creatures.
They have a pocket and they collect rocks and use them as tools for many things.

The goal is to make this website more like a digital living corner where
guests, who happens to browse and stop by, can learn engage with me, then hopefully
leave a touch of their digital footprint.

I thought about how personal websites should behave more like social media
feeds rather than having to keep up with platforms like Twitter or Instagram.
